## 1. What this project is

A browser-based asynchronous multiplayer strategy game for a closed friend group of history enthusiasts ("the gentlemen"). Up to 5 players each control a historical empire competing over roughly 200–400 in-game years across 8–40 turns. Turns resolve on a 48-hour window with Discord bot notifications.

The central design question — and the philosophical premise the entire mechanical system is built to express — is:

> **"Which empire in history would you actually prefer to be conquered by?"**

Victory is not measured by territorial dominance. It is measured by a **Legitimacy Score** that rewards governance quality, prosperity of subjects, and successful assimilation. A small, well-governed empire can and should beat a vast unstable one. Conquest is easy; making conquered people accept their fate is hard.

### The audience and the project shape

This is being built for a specific friend group, not a general audience. That has consequences for development priorities:

- Polish matters less than the system being **legible and fair** to people who care about history
- The game must support a **formal, witty register** in its text output (announcement copy, event descriptions, Discord narration) — this is the group's communication style
- Content is **friend-authored**: empires, abilities, and historical events come from the contributor portal rather than being hardcoded
- The dev team's job is to build the **engine and infrastructure**. The friends supply the world

### Co-development model

- **Mark** owns game design and friend-group communication
- **The friends** contribute historical content (empires, events) through a non-technical web portal
- **The AI dev team (you)** owns implementation — backend, game logic, Discord bot, frontend
- **Claude in conversation** is Mark's design partner — iterates on GDD versions, JSON content, copy

When the dev team encounters a design ambiguity, the default is to ask Mark rather than invent. The GDD is authoritative; this document is authoritative; everything else needs confirmation.

## 2. Current state of the design

### What is settled (GDD v0.5)

**Resource system** — Three resource pools, generated by territories and multiplied by empire stats. Replaced an earlier Action Point system that was felt to be too flat.

- **Wealth** — trade, cities, tribute → buildings, diplomacy, recruitment
- **Manpower** — population, fertile regions → armies, conquest, upkeep
- **Influence** — culture, religious institutions, courts → assimilation, diplomatic actions, abilities

**Stats** — Four stats summing to exactly 14 points. Stats simultaneously (a) set the generation rate of the relevant resource pool from controlled territories and (b) discount action costs via the formula:

> `Final action cost = Base cost − (relevant stat − 3)`

So a stat of 3 is neutral, 5 is a strong discount, 1 is a penalty. This makes every stat point a meaningful design decision when submitting an empire.

| Stat | Resource generation | Action discount on |
|---|---|---|
| Military | Manpower | Conquer, Suppress |
| Economy | Wealth | Invest, Build |
| Culture | Influence | Assimilate, Empire Ability |
| Diplomacy | Influence (secondary) | Diplomacy, Cultural Exchange |

**Governing Philosophies** — Each empire belongs to one of five philosophies. These are not flavour — they materially change victory conditions, the shape of crises the empire is vulnerable to, action preferences, and which event response choices are discounted. Era-neutral event design (see §3) means no era should structurally favour any one philosophy.

- **The Sword** — legitimacy through force and order
- **The Pen** — legitimacy through institutions, law, learning
- **The Coin** — legitimacy through trade and prosperity
- **The Word** — legitimacy through religion or ideology
- **The Thread** — legitimacy through diplomacy and network

**Territory assimilation** — Three stages with concrete mechanical consequences:

| Stage | Resource efficiency | Rebellion risk | Legitimacy contribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Occupied | 50% | High | Negative |
| Integrated | 75% | Medium | Neutral |
| Assimilated | 100% | Low | Positive |

Progression happens automatically over turns and can be accelerated by Invest actions, empire abilities, and low cultural distance.

**Strain & Collapse** — Empires accumulate Strain from Occupied territory ratio, negative resource balances, active rebellions, and stagnation. At 60% Strain, the empire enters a **Stressed** state with visible warnings (Discord DM, reduced AP, slowed Resistance ticks) giving the player 2–3 turns to react. At 100%, the empire collapses: Occupied territories revert to independent, Integrated territories break away on a 50% roll, Assimilated territories independently roll 50% to stay with the warlord or become inert Successor States. The player transitions to **warlord status** — still in the game, still capable of deciding who wins. Collapse is fast but always foreshadowed.

**Warlord status** — A collapsed empire keeps its capital and some territories, retains its identity, but can no longer win the main Legitimacy race. Warlords can invest, suppress, trade, and **pledge vassalage** to any major empire through publicly negotiated three-term agreements: resource share (25–75%), duration lock (0–5 turns), and one named guarantee (Protection / Tribute / Honour). The Verdict of History names the **Maker of Kings** — the warlord whose allegiances most shaped the outcome.

**Empire abilities** — Each empire has a passive (always on) and an active (5-turn cooldown). Designed to express historical character, not generic bonuses.

**End conditions** — Two ways to win before turn limit:

- **Concession Vote** — triggered when one empire holds a 30%+ Legitimacy lead for three consecutive turns; resolved by majority vote of all surviving major empires (warlords do not vote)
- **Runaway Victory** — auto-triggered if an empire reaches 35%+ Legitimacy lead, has zero Occupied territories, and has positive generation across all three resource pools

**Map** — Full world map, divided into five continental zones: Europe, Americas, Africa, Middle East / South Asia, East Asia. Zones connect only via ocean tiles, which require the **Oceanic Threshold** (a stable coastal port + minimum Wealth + minimum Influence) to cross. The first crossing of any game is a major public narrative event.

**Era brackets** — Three planned: Ancient (~500 BC – 500 AD), Medieval (~700 – 1300 AD), Early Modern (1300 – 1648 AD). Launch product ships Early Modern only. An empire is available in a bracket if its historical lifespan overlaps that bracket.

**Diplomacy channels** — All formal actions (deal proposals, pledge agreements) happen inside the game UI. All conversational chatter, threats, and side bargaining happen on Discord. The game UI handles formal actions; Discord handles everything else.

**Turn resolution visibility** — After a turn resolves, every player sees every submitted action and its outcome. The one exception: rejected diplomatic proposals remain private to sender and recipient.

**Event system** — Two event types, both data-driven:

- **Anchor events** — fire on specific years, regardless of game state
- **Ripple events** — fire when game-state conditions are met

Both include: chronicle text, mechanical effect, response choices, philosophy-specific differential, and Discord announcement string. The first complete event pool (Early Modern, 1300–1648) is delivered as `imperium-events.json`. Additional era pools are on the roadmap.

## 3. Design principles — read before adding anything

These principles have been arrived at through iteration and pushback. They override your instincts about what would be a "cool feature."

### Elegant simplicity over layered complexity

The design philosophy is **mechanics that generate historical stories, not spreadsheet management**. Two specific proposals — Secret Objectives and Opportunist Windows — were considered and rejected for adding complexity without generating better stories. If you find yourself proposing a new mechanic, the question to ask is: does this produce a better story at the table, or does it just produce more numbers?

When in doubt, prefer fewer moving parts.

### Emergent narrative over special-case rules

Surprising outcomes — an underdog comeback, a sudden collapse of the leader, a diplomatic betrayal that reshapes the map — should arise from the interaction of existing systems, not from bespoke mechanics added to produce those moments. If a desirable behaviour requires a new rule, first ask whether existing rules can be tuned to produce it instead.

### Era neutrality in event design

Event pools must be **balanced across all five Governing Philosophies regardless of era**. No era should structurally favour Sword over Pen, or Word over Thread, etc. When building new event pools, audit the philosophy_impact fields across the whole pool to confirm rough parity. Era flavour is welcome; era structural bias is not.

### Data-driven architecture, no hardcoding

Empires, events, regions, and map data are **never hardcoded into the engine**. Everything loads from JSON contributed through the portal. This is non-negotiable — it keeps the system extensible and means the friends can shape the world without developer intervention. If you find yourself wanting to hardcode something for convenience, instead define the schema and load it.

### Visible consequences

Strain warnings, ripple event triggers, rebellion build-up — all of these should be **visible to the player before they bite**. The drama is in seeing the cliff edge coming, not in being shoved off it. Avoid hidden state that determines outcomes the player couldn't have anticipated.

### Tone and register

In-game text — Discord narration, event chronicles, collapse summaries, turn announcements — should hit a **formal and witty register**, suited to a group of history enthusiasts. Look at the chronicle and flavour_quote fields in `imperium-events.json` for the target tone. Don't write like a video game tutorial; write like a 19th-century historian with a sense of humour.

## 5. Data schemas — what the engine consumes

### Empire schema (from contributor portal)

Fields collected by the portal form:

- `type: "empire"`
- `author` — submitter name
- `name` — empire name
- `from`, `until` — active year range (used to filter by chosen era at game setup)
- `regions` — comma-separated home regions, 1–3
- `continental_zone` — enum: `Europe`, `Americas`, `Africa`, `Middle_East_South_Asia`, `East_Asia`
- `era_bracket` — array of enums: `Ancient`, `Medieval`, `Early_Modern` (multi-select; an empire can overlap multiple brackets)
- `philosophy` — one-sentence governing philosophy (flavour text)
- `governing_philosophy` — dropdown selection from: `Sword`, `Pen`, `Coin`, `Word`, `Thread` ✓ confirmed; contributor portal extended with an explicit dropdown for this field
- `stats` — `{ military, economy, culture, diplomacy }`, integers, must sum to 14
- `passive` — passive ability, plain English
- `active` — active ability, plain English (5-turn cooldown)
- `affinity` — cultural affinity description (regions that accept this empire's rule most easily)
- `collapse` — optional dramatic collapse flavour text
- `notes` — historical notes that should influence mechanics
- `timestamp` — millis since epoch

### Anchor event schema

The `imperium-events.json` shape is the authoritative spec. Key fields:

- `id`, `name`, `year`, `affected` (string describing scope)
- `chronicle` — atmospheric text shown when the event fires
- `mechanical_effect` — structured object with `summary`, `duration_turns`, modifier arrays, scaling rules
- `response_choices` — array of 3 (typically) `{ id, label, description, cost, benefit }` objects offered to affected empires
- `differential` — prose description of how the event impacts different philosophies asymmetrically
- `philosophy_impact` — machine-readable mirror of the differential, keyed by philosophy
- `flavour_quote` — optional historical quote

The portal as currently built captures a flatter version of this (name, year, affected, description, effect, differential, quote). The dev team will need a translation layer between portal submissions and the richer engine format, OR the portal needs to be extended to capture response choices and philosophy impact directly. Recommend the latter — discuss with Mark.

### Ripple event schema

Similar to anchor events but with `trigger_condition` (a structured rule set) instead of `year`, and `frequency` (once per game / once per empire / every N turns).

The structured trigger format in `imperium-events.json` uses a rules array supporting `operator: "OR"`, comparison operators, and named conditions like `anchor_printing_press_has_fired`. The engine needs a rule evaluator that can read these and check them against game state at the end of each turn's Phase 5.

### Region/map schema

Each region needs:

- `id`, `name`, `continental_zone`
- `adjacencies` — array of region IDs within the same zone
- `ocean_adjacencies` — array of region IDs in other zones, traversable only when the Oceanic Threshold is met
- `base_resources` — `{ wealth_generation, manpower_generation, influence_generation }`
- `population_size` — enum: `Small`, `Medium`, `Large`
- `cultural_identity` — array of cultural identity tags
- `strategic_value` — array of flags: `trade_hub`, `fertile_heartland`, `mountain_pass`, `coastal`, etc.
- `starting_owner` — empire ID, `"independent"`, or null

**This is a critical gap.** Approximately 50 regions across five zones with full adjacency graphs and ocean edges must be authored before the engine can run a game. This is a content task, not an engineering task — it can be friend-group authored through an extended portal form or directly as JSON. Flag to Mark.

## 6. Turn engine — phase order

Each turn resolves five phases in order, server-side, after all players have submitted (or the 48-hour window expires):

1. **The Chronicle** — display current year; fire anchor events for this turn; affected players see response choices on their dashboard
2. **Resource Collection** — every empire collects Wealth, Manpower, Influence from its territories, modified by assimilation stage, active events, passive abilities, stat multipliers
3. **Stability Update** — Resistance ticks in territories based on empire behaviour. Strain indicators recalculate. Rebellions trigger if Resistance is critical
4. **Player Actions** — all submitted actions resolved simultaneously. Action costs deducted with stat discount formula. Conquest, Invest, Build, Diplomacy, Assimilate, Suppress, Contract, Empire Ability, Warlord Pledge
5. **Score & Strain Update** — Legitimacy recalculated. Ripple events evaluated against new game state and fire if triggered. Turn resolution summary generated (narrated, not enumerated) and posted to Discord. Next turn opens

Simultaneous resolution means action conflicts (two empires trying to conquer the same territory in the same turn) need a deterministic resolution order. **Not yet specified — flag to Mark before Milestone 2.**

## 7. The Discord bot

The bot is the game's primary communication layer. Mechanically minimal but tonally critical — the bot is the **voice of the chronicle**. Treat its output as in-character historian-narrator text, not system notifications. The bot narrates rather than enumerates: *"The Ottoman army crossed the Danube in force, but Habsburg defences held the line at Mohács"* — not `Ottoman: Conquer(Hungary). Result: failed.`

Required bot behaviours:

- "Turn N is now open — you have 48 hours" when a turn opens
- Private DMs to individual players when their attention is needed (Strain warning, diplomatic proposal received, response choice needed for an anchor event)
- "⚠ 12 hours remaining — [Player] has not yet submitted" deadline reminder
- Full turn resolution summary posted publicly when a turn resolves (narrated voice)
- Major event announcements drawn from the `discord_announcement` field on each event
- Collapse announcements — dramatic, public, drawn from the empire's `collapse` flavour field if present
- Pledge agreement announcements — public, including the three terms
- First ocean crossing — special announcement, chronicle voice

Setup flow: bot is invited to the friend group's existing Discord server and configured with a designated game channel at game creation.

When iterating with Mark on this project:

- **Default to confirming, not inventing.** If a design point isn't specified, ask
- **Push back if you think a direction is wrong**, but reset cleanly when Mark corrects course. Mark has pushed back on over-engineering before and will again
